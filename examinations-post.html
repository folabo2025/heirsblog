The Season of Harvest: Navigating the 2026 Second Term Examinations

Published on February 23, 2026 ‚Ä¢ By Office of the Registrar ‚Ä¢ 10 min read

As we approach the twilight of the second term, the halls of Heirs of Promise Schools are transforming into sanctuaries of focus and intellectual rigor. The Second Term Examinations are not merely a test of memory, but a "Season of Harvest" where students showcase the seeds of knowledge sown since January.

This term is traditionally the most critical academic stretch of the year. It serves as the bridge between foundational concepts and the advanced applications required for final promotion. Our faculty has worked tirelessly to ensure that every student is equipped with the mental tools to excel.

Standardized Assessment Framework

In line with our commitment to global standards, the 2026 Second Term papers have been moderated to test four key domains: Critical Thinking, Analytical Writing, Computational Accuracy, and Practical Application.

  • Junior School: Emphasis on integrated science and numeracy foundations.
  • Senior School: Exam patterns modeled after WAEC and SAT standards to prepare students for high-stakes external certifications.

Exam Protocols & Integrity

We maintain a zero-tolerance policy toward examination malpractice. Our halls are equipped with advanced monitoring, and our invigilation team is trained to maintain a serene, fair, and orderly environment. We encourage students to rely on their preparation, as true success is the only success that lasts.

üí° The HOPS "Ace-it" Strategy

  1. The 48-Hour Rule: Complete all intensive reading 48 hours before the paper to allow the brain to move information into long-term memory.
  2. Hydration & Sleep: Scientific studies show that a well-rested brain performs 30% better in logic-based tasks.
  3. Past Question Immersion: Use the E-Library portal to access five years of previous HOPS second-term papers.

Important Notice for Parents

To ensure a seamless examination experience for your child, we kindly request that all outstanding Second Term Tuition Fees be settled. Examination dockets (Admit Cards) will be issued starting Monday, March 2nd, to students with cleared accounts.
